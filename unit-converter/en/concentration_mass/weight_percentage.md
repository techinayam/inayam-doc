## Weight Percentage Tool Description

### Definition
The weight percentage, denoted as %wt, is a unit of concentration that expresses the mass of a solute as a percentage of the total mass of the solution. This measurement is crucial in various fields, including chemistry, biology, and food science, as it helps in quantifying the concentration of substances in a solution.

### Standardization
The weight percentage is standardized across scientific disciplines, ensuring consistency and accuracy in measurements. It is calculated using the formula:

\[ \text{Weight Percentage (%wt)} = \left( \frac{\text{Mass of Solute}}{\text{Total Mass of Solution}} \right) \times 100 \]

This standardization allows for easy comparison and replication of results in research and industry applications.

### History and Evolution
The concept of weight percentage has been utilized since the early days of chemistry. As scientific understanding advanced, the need for precise measurements led to the formalization of concentration units, including weight percentage. Over time, this unit has evolved to become a fundamental aspect of quantitative analysis in laboratories and industries worldwide.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate the calculation of weight percentage, consider a solution containing 5 grams of salt dissolved in 95 grams of water. The total mass of the solution is 100 grams. The weight percentage of salt in the solution can be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Weight Percentage of Salt} = \left( \frac{5 \text{ g}}{100 \text{ g}} \right) \times 100 = 5\% \]
