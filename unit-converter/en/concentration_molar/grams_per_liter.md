## Grams per Liter (g/L) Tool Description

### Definition
Grams per liter (g/L) is a unit of concentration that measures the mass of a substance (in grams) present in one liter of solution. This metric is widely used in various scientific fields, including chemistry, biology, and environmental science, to quantify the concentration of solutes in solutions. Understanding g/L is crucial for accurate measurements in laboratory settings and industrial applications.

### Standardization
The grams per liter unit is standardized under the International System of Units (SI). It provides a consistent method for measuring concentration, ensuring that results are comparable across different studies and applications. This standardization is essential for researchers and professionals who rely on precise measurements for their work.

### History and Evolution
The concept of measuring concentration dates back to the early days of chemistry when scientists sought to understand the properties of solutions. The use of grams per liter became more prevalent with the development of modern analytical techniques in the 19th and 20th centuries. Today, g/L is a fundamental unit in various scientific disciplines, facilitating communication and collaboration among researchers worldwide.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate how to convert a concentration from moles per liter (mol/L) to grams per liter (g/L), consider a solution of sodium chloride (NaCl) with a molarity of 1 mol/L. The molar mass of NaCl is approximately 58.44 g/mol. The calculation would be as follows:

\[ \text{Concentration (g/L)} = \text{Molarity (mol/L)} \times \text{Molar Mass (g/mol)} \]
\[ \text{Concentration (g/L)} = 1 \, \text{mol/L} \times 58.44 \, \text{g/mol} = 58.44 \, \text{g/L} \]
