## Gigawatt Hour (GWh) Converter Tool

### Definition
A gigawatt hour (GWh) is a unit of energy equivalent to one billion watt-hours. It is commonly used to measure large-scale energy production and consumption, particularly in the context of electricity generation and usage. Understanding GWh is crucial for energy professionals, policymakers, and consumers alike, as it provides insight into the efficiency and sustainability of energy systems.

### Standardization
The gigawatt hour is part of the International System of Units (SI) and is standardized to ensure consistency across various applications. It is derived from the watt, which is the basic unit of power, and the hour, a unit of time. This standardization allows for accurate comparisons and calculations in energy production, consumption, and storage.

### History and Evolution
The concept of measuring energy in terms of watt-hours dates back to the late 19th century when electricity began to be generated and distributed on a large scale. The gigawatt hour emerged as a practical unit for quantifying energy output from power plants, particularly in the context of renewable energy sources like wind and solar. As global energy demands have increased, the relevance of GWh has grown, making it a vital metric in discussions about energy efficiency and sustainability.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate how to convert energy measurements, consider a power plant that generates 2 gigawatts (GW) of electricity over a period of 5 hours. The total energy produced can be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Energy (GWh)} = \text{Power (GW)} \times \text{Time (h)} \]

\[ \text{Energy (GWh)} = 2 \, \text{GW} \times 5 \, \text{h} = 10 \, \text{GWh} \]
