### Definition
A quart per second (qt/s) measures the volume of liquid flowing per second, specifically in quarts. One quart is equivalent to approximately 0.946 liters. This unit is commonly used in culinary, industrial, and scientific applications to ensure precise measurements of liquid flow.

### Standardization
The quart is a unit of volume that is standardized in both the US customary and imperial systems. In the US, one quart is equal to 32 fluid ounces, while in the UK, it is approximately 40 fluid ounces. The quart per second is therefore standardized based on these definitions, ensuring consistency across various applications.

### History and Evolution
The quart has its origins in the Latin word "quartus," meaning "fourth." Historically, it was used to measure a quarter of a gallon. Over time, as the need for precise liquid measurement grew, the quart evolved into a standard unit of measurement in various fields, including cooking, chemistry, and engineering. The quart per second has become increasingly important in modern applications, particularly in industries that require accurate flow rate measurements.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate the use of the quart per second, consider a scenario where a pump is delivering water at a rate of 2 qt/s. To convert this flow rate into liters per second, you would multiply by the conversion factor (0.946 liters per quart):

\[ 
2 \, qt/s \times 0.946 \, L/qt = 1.892 \, L/s 
\]
