## Kilogram Force Meter (kgf·m) Tool Description

### Definition
The kilogram force meter (kgf·m) is a unit of torque that represents the torque produced by a force of one kilogram acting at a distance of one meter from the pivot point. This measurement is essential in various fields such as engineering, physics, and mechanics, where understanding the relationship between force and distance is crucial for calculating rotational effects.

### Standardization
The kilogram force meter is part of the metric system and is standardized under the International System of Units (SI). While the SI unit for torque is the newton meter (N·m), the kilogram force meter is often used in practical applications, especially in regions that still utilize the metric system for engineering and mechanical calculations.

### History and Evolution
The concept of torque has been around since ancient times, but the formalization of units like the kilogram force meter emerged during the 19th century as the metric system gained popularity. The kgf·m has evolved alongside advancements in physics and engineering, providing a practical way to express rotational force in a straightforward manner.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate how to use the kilogram force meter, consider a scenario where a force of 5 kg is applied at a distance of 2 meters from a pivot point. The torque can be calculated as follows:
\[ \text{Torque (kgf·m)} = \text{Force (kg)} \times \text{Distance (m)} \]
\[ \text{Torque} = 5 \, \text{kg} \times 2 \, \text{m} = 10 \, \text{kgf·m} \]

### Use of the Units
