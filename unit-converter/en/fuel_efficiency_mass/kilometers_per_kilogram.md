## Tool Description: Kilometers per Kilogram (km/kg) Converter

The **Kilometers per Kilogram (km/kg)** converter is a specialized tool designed to help users calculate fuel efficiency in terms of distance traveled per unit of mass. This measurement is particularly useful in various fields, including automotive engineering, transportation logistics, and environmental studies. By converting kilometers per kilogram, users can gain insights into the efficiency of different vehicles or transportation methods, allowing for better decision-making and resource management.

### Definition

Kilometers per kilogram (km/kg) is a unit of measurement that expresses the distance traveled (in kilometers) for every kilogram of fuel consumed. This metric is essential for evaluating the efficiency of vehicles, especially in terms of fuel consumption and environmental impact.

### Standardization

The km/kg unit is part of the metric system, which is standardized globally. It allows for consistent measurements across various applications, making it easier to compare the fuel efficiency of different vehicles or methods of transportation.

### History and Evolution

The concept of measuring fuel efficiency has evolved significantly over the years. Initially, fuel consumption was measured in terms of liters per 100 kilometers (L/100 km). However, as environmental concerns grew, the need for more efficient metrics led to the adoption of kilometers per kilogram, which provides a clearer picture of fuel efficiency relative to weight.

### Example Calculation

To illustrate how to use the kilometers per kilogram converter, consider a vehicle that travels 300 kilometers while consuming 15 kilograms of fuel. The calculation would be:

\[ \text{Fuel Efficiency} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Fuel Consumed}} = \frac{300 \text{ km}}{15 \text{ kg}} = 20 \text{ km/kg} \]

This means the vehicle travels 20 kilometers for every kilogram of fuel consumed.
