### Definition

Miles per gallon (mpg) is a unit of measurement used to express the distance a vehicle can travel on one gallon of fuel. It is a critical metric for evaluating fuel efficiency, helping consumers make informed decisions when purchasing vehicles or planning trips.

### Standardization

The mpg unit is standardized in the United States, where it is commonly used to assess the fuel economy of cars and trucks. In other regions, such as Europe, fuel efficiency is often measured in liters per 100 kilometers (L/100km). Our tool allows users to seamlessly convert between these units, ensuring clarity and consistency in fuel efficiency measurements.

### History and Evolution

The concept of measuring fuel efficiency dates back to the early 20th century when automobiles became widely popular. Initially, fuel consumption was measured in terms of distance traveled per unit of fuel consumed. Over time, the mpg metric became the standard in the U.S., reflecting the growing importance of fuel efficiency in an era of rising fuel prices and environmental awareness.

### Example Calculation

To illustrate how to use the mpg tool, consider a vehicle that can travel 300 miles on 10 gallons of fuel. To calculate the mpg, you would divide the distance by the gallons used:

\[ \text{mpg} = \frac{\text{Distance (miles)}}{\text{Fuel (gallons)}} = \frac{300 \text{ miles}}{10 \text{ gallons}} = 30 \text{ mpg} \]
