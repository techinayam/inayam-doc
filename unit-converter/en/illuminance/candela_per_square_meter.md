## Candela per Square Meter (cd/m²) Tool Description

### Definition
The candela per square meter (cd/m²), commonly referred to as "nits," is a unit of measurement for luminance. It quantifies the amount of light emitted from a surface in a specific direction, per unit area. This measurement is crucial for assessing the brightness of displays, lighting fixtures, and other surfaces that emit light.

### Standardization
The candela per square meter is standardized by the International System of Units (SI) and is derived from the base unit of luminous intensity, the candela (cd). One candela per square meter is equivalent to one candela of luminous intensity emitted from a surface area of one square meter. This standardization ensures consistency in measuring brightness across various applications.

### History and Evolution
The concept of luminance has evolved significantly since the early days of photometry. The candela was defined in the late 19th century, and the term "candela per square meter" was introduced as a way to quantify brightness in a more practical manner. Over the years, advancements in technology have led to the widespread use of this measurement in various fields, including television, computer displays, and architectural lighting.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate the use of the candela per square meter, consider a light source that emits 1000 candelas uniformly across a surface area of 2 square meters. The luminance can be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Luminance (cd/m²)} = \frac{\text{Luminous Intensity (cd)}}{\text{Area (m²)}} \]

\[ \text{Luminance} = \frac{1000 \, \text{cd}}{2 \, \text{m²}} = 500 \, \text{cd/m²} \]

This calculation shows that the surface has a luminance of 500 cd/m².

- **Lighting Design**: To evaluate the effectiveness of lighting fixtures in illuminating spaces.
