## Lumen per Square Centimeter (lm/cm²) Tool Description

### Definition
Lumen per square centimeter (lm/cm²) is a unit of measurement that quantifies illuminance, representing the amount of luminous flux (light) received on a surface area of one square centimeter. This metric is crucial in various fields, including photography, architecture, and interior design, where proper lighting is essential for aesthetics and functionality.

### Standardization
The lumen is the standard unit of luminous flux in the International System of Units (SI). One lumen is defined as the amount of light emitted in a unit solid angle by a point source of light with a uniform intensity of one candela. The standardization of illuminance measurements ensures consistency across different applications and industries.

### History and Evolution
The concept of measuring light dates back to the early 19th century when scientists began to explore the properties of light and its effects on human vision. The lumen was formally defined in the early 20th century, leading to the development of various illuminance units, including lumen per square centimeter. Over the years, advancements in lighting technology and measurement techniques have refined our understanding of light and its applications.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate the use of lumen per square centimeter, consider a light source that emits 1000 lumens evenly across a surface area of 100 square centimeters. The illuminance can be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Illuminance (lm/cm²)} = \frac{\text{Total Lumens}}{\text{Area (cm²)}} \]

\[ \text{Illuminance} = \frac{1000 \, \text{lm}}{100 \, \text{cm²}} = 10 \, \text{lm/cm²} \]
