## Lumen per Watt (lm/W) Converter Tool

### Definition
Lumen per watt (lm/W) is a unit of measurement that quantifies the efficiency of a light source by comparing the amount of visible light (in lumens) it produces to the amount of energy (in watts) it consumes. This metric is crucial for evaluating the performance of lighting systems, helping consumers and professionals make informed decisions about energy-efficient lighting solutions.

### Standardization
The lumen per watt metric is standardized globally, allowing for consistent comparisons across different types of light sources, including incandescent, fluorescent, and LED lights. This standardization ensures that users can easily assess the energy efficiency of various lighting options, promoting sustainability and cost savings.

### History and Evolution
The concept of measuring light output relative to energy consumption has evolved significantly since the introduction of electric lighting. Initially, incandescent bulbs dominated the market, but as energy costs rose and environmental concerns grew, more efficient lighting technologies emerged. The development of compact fluorescent lamps (CFLs) and light-emitting diodes (LEDs) has revolutionized the industry, making the lumen per watt metric increasingly important for consumers seeking to reduce their energy consumption.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate the use of the lumen per watt metric, consider a light bulb that produces 800 lumens and consumes 10 watts of power. The efficiency can be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Efficiency (lm/W)} = \frac{\text{Lumens}}{\text{Watts}} = \frac{800 \text{ lm}}{10 \text{ W}} = 80 \text{ lm/W} \]

This means that the bulb provides 80 lumens of light for every watt of energy consumed, indicating a relatively efficient lighting option.
