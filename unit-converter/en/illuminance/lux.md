## Lux (lx) - Illuminance Unit Converter

### Definition
Lux (lx) is the SI unit of illuminance, measuring the amount of light that falls on a surface per unit area. Specifically, one lux is defined as one lumen per square meter. This measurement is crucial in various fields, including photography, architecture, and horticulture, where understanding light intensity is essential for optimal outcomes.

### Standardization
The lux unit is standardized under the International System of Units (SI). It is widely recognized and utilized across various industries, ensuring consistency in measuring light levels. This standardization allows professionals to communicate effectively about lighting conditions, making it easier to design spaces that meet specific lighting requirements.

### History and Evolution
The concept of measuring light has evolved significantly over the years. The term "lux" was introduced in the early 20th century as part of the metric system. Before the adoption of lux, light intensity was often measured in foot-candles, which is still used in some regions. The transition to lux has provided a more precise and universally accepted method for measuring illuminance, facilitating advancements in lighting technology and design.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate how lux is calculated, consider a scenario where a light source emits 1000 lumens over an area of 10 square meters. The illuminance in lux can be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Illuminance (lx)} = \frac{\text{Total Lumens}}{\text{Area (m}^2\text{)}} \]

\[ \text{Illuminance (lx)} = \frac{1000 \text{ lumens}}{10 \text{ m}^2} = 100 \text{ lx} \]

This calculation shows that the area receives an illuminance of 100 lux.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**1. What is lux in lighting?**  
Lux is the unit of illuminance that measures the amount of light that falls on a surface per square meter.

**2. How do I convert lux to lumens?**  
To convert lux to lumens, multiply the lux value by the area in square meters. The formula is:  
\[ \text{Lumens} = \text{Lux} \times \text{Area (m}^2\text{)} \]

**3. What is the difference between lux and foot-candle?**  
Lux is the metric unit of illuminance, while foot-candle is the imperial unit. One foot-candle is approximately equal to 10.764 lux.

**4. How much lux is needed for a typical office?**  
A typical office environment requires around 300 to 500 lux for comfortable working conditions.
