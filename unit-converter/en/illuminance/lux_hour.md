## Lux-Hour: Your Ultimate Tool for Illuminance Measurement

### Definition
The lux-hour (lx·h) is a unit of measurement that quantifies the amount of light received over a period of time. It is derived from the lux, which measures illuminance, and is crucial for assessing lighting conditions in various environments. Understanding lux-hours is essential for architects, interior designers, and anyone concerned with optimal lighting.

### Standardization
The lux-hour is standardized under the International System of Units (SI). One lux is defined as one lumen per square meter, and when you multiply this by time in hours, you obtain lux-hours. This standardization ensures that measurements are consistent and universally understood, making it easier to compare lighting conditions across different settings.

### History and Evolution
The concept of measuring light dates back to the early 20th century when scientists began to understand the importance of light in various applications. The lux was officially defined in 1948, and since then, the lux-hour has evolved as a practical tool for measuring light exposure over time. This evolution has led to its widespread use in fields such as photography, agriculture, and health sciences.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate how to calculate lux-hours, consider a scenario where a workspace is illuminated with a light source providing 500 lux for 3 hours. The calculation would be as follows:

\[ \text{Lux-Hours} = \text{Illuminance (lux)} \times \text{Time (hours)} \]
\[ \text{Lux-Hours} = 500 \, \text{lx} \times 3 \, \text{h} = 1500 \, \text{lx·h} \]

This means that the workspace received a total of 1500 lux-hours of light during that period.
