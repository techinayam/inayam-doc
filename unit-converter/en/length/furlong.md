### Definition
The furlong is a unit of length that is primarily used in horse racing and other equestrian sports. One furlong is equivalent to 1/8 of a mile, or 220 yards. The symbol for furlong is "fur." This unit is not commonly used in everyday measurements but holds significance in specific contexts, particularly in the United States and the United Kingdom.

### Standardization
The furlong is part of the imperial system of measurement and is standardized as exactly 201.168 meters. This standardization ensures consistency across various applications, making it easier for users to convert furlongs into other units of length, such as kilometers or meters.

### History and Evolution
The term "furlong" originates from the Old English term "furhlang," which means "the length of a furrow." Historically, it was defined as the distance a team of oxen could plow in one day. Over time, the furlong became standardized and is now primarily used in horse racing, where distances are often measured in furlongs.
