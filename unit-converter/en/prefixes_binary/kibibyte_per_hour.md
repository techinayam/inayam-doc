## Kibibyte per Hour (KiB/h) Tool Description

### Definition
The Kibibyte per Hour (KiB/h) is a unit of measurement that quantifies data transfer rates, specifically indicating how many kibibytes (KiB) of data are transmitted or processed in one hour. This metric is particularly relevant in the fields of computing and data management, where understanding data transfer speeds is crucial for optimizing performance.

### Standardization
The Kibibyte is a binary unit of data measurement, standardized by the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC). One kibibyte is equal to 1024 bytes. The use of KiB/h allows for a clear understanding of data rates in environments where binary calculations are preferred, such as in computer science and information technology.

### History and Evolution
The concept of measuring data transfer rates has evolved significantly with the advancement of technology. Initially, data rates were often expressed in bits per second (bps), but as data storage and processing capabilities grew, more granular units like the kibibyte emerged. The adoption of the kibibyte and its derivatives (like KiB/h) has become essential for accurately representing data transfer in modern computing environments.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate the use of the Kibibyte per Hour, consider a scenario where a server transfers 2048 KiB of data in two hours. To calculate the transfer rate in KiB/h, divide the total data by the total time:
- Total Data: 2048 KiB
- Total Time: 2 hours
- Calculation: 2048 KiB / 2 hours = 1024 KiB/h
