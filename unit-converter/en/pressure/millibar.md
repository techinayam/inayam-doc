## Millibar (mbar) Tool Description

The millibar (mbar) is a unit of pressure that is widely used in meteorology and various scientific applications. It is defined as one-thousandth of a bar, where one bar is equivalent to 100,000 pascals (Pa). The millibar is particularly useful for measuring atmospheric pressure, making it an essential tool for weather forecasting and climate studies.

### Standardization

The millibar is standardized under the International System of Units (SI) and is commonly used in conjunction with other pressure units such as pascals (Pa) and bars. The conversion between these units is straightforward: 1 mbar equals 100 pascals. This standardization ensures that measurements are consistent and reliable across various scientific disciplines.

### History and Evolution

The millibar was introduced in the early 20th century as a practical unit for meteorological observations. It gained popularity due to its convenience in expressing atmospheric pressure, especially since the average sea-level pressure is approximately 1013.25 mbar. Over the years, the millibar has become a staple in weather reports and scientific research, evolving alongside advancements in technology and measurement techniques.
