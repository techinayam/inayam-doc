## Stagnation Pressure Tool Description

### Definition
Stagnation pressure, measured in Pascals (Pa), is a crucial concept in fluid dynamics. It represents the pressure a fluid would attain if brought to rest isentropically (without heat transfer). This measurement is essential in various engineering applications, particularly in aerodynamics and hydrodynamics, where understanding the behavior of fluids under different conditions is vital.

### Standardization
Stagnation pressure is standardized in the International System of Units (SI) and is expressed in Pascals (Pa). This unit is derived from the basic SI units of force and area, where 1 Pascal equals 1 Newton per square meter. The standardization of pressure measurements allows for consistency and accuracy across scientific and engineering disciplines.
