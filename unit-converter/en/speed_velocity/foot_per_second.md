### Definition

Foot per second (ft/s) is a unit of speed that indicates the distance traveled in feet within one second. It is part of the Imperial system of measurement and is widely used in the United States for various applications, including sports, automotive engineering, and physics.

### Standardization

The foot is standardized as 0.3048 meters in the International System of Units (SI). Consequently, the foot per second can be converted to other speed units, such as meters per second (m/s) or kilometers per hour (km/h), using this standard conversion factor.
