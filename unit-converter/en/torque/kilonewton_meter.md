## Kilonewton Meter (kN·m) Tool Description

### Definition
The kilonewton meter (kN·m) is a unit of torque, a measure of the rotational force applied at a distance from a pivot point. Torque is crucial in various engineering and mechanical applications, as it determines the effectiveness of rotational motion. One kilonewton meter is equivalent to a force of one kilonewton applied at a distance of one meter from the pivot point.

### Standardization
The kilonewton meter is part of the International System of Units (SI), where it is standardized for use in scientific and engineering calculations. This unit is widely accepted in fields such as mechanical engineering, automotive design, and construction, ensuring consistency and accuracy in measurements across various applications.

### History and Evolution
The concept of torque has been studied for centuries, with early applications in mechanics dating back to ancient civilizations. The kilonewton meter emerged as a standardized unit in the 20th century, coinciding with advancements in engineering and technology. As industries evolved, the need for precise torque measurements became paramount, leading to the adoption of kN·m as a reliable unit of measurement.

### Example Calculation
To illustrate the use of kilonewton meters, consider a scenario where a force of 5 kN is applied at a distance of 2 meters from a pivot point. The torque can be calculated using the formula:

\[ \text{Torque (kN·m)} = \text{Force (kN)} \times \text{Distance (m)} \]

Thus, the torque would be:

\[ \text{Torque} = 5 \, \text{kN} \times 2 \, \text{m} = 10 \, \text{kN·m} \]
