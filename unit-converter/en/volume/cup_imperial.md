### Definition

A cup is a unit of volume commonly used in cooking and serving sizes. The imperial cup, specifically, is defined as 284.131 milliliters. This measurement is widely recognized in the UK and is essential for standardizing recipes across different regions.

Standardization of the cup measurement is crucial for consistency in cooking and baking. The imperial cup differs from the US cup, which is approximately 236.588 milliliters. Understanding these differences ensures that recipes yield the expected results, regardless of the measurement system used.
