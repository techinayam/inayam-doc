## Fluid Ounce (Imperial) Converter Tool

### Definition
The fluid ounce (imperial) is a unit of volume commonly used in the United Kingdom and other countries that follow the imperial system. It is abbreviated as "fl oz" and is primarily used to measure liquids. One imperial fluid ounce is equivalent to approximately 28.41 milliliters, making it a crucial unit in cooking, beverage serving, and scientific measurements.

### Standardization
The imperial fluid ounce is standardized under the imperial measurement system, which was established in the 19th century. This standardization ensures consistency in measurements across various applications, such as culinary recipes, laboratory experiments, and industrial processes.

### History and Evolution
The fluid ounce has its roots in the early measurements of volume, dating back to the medieval period. It evolved from the various measurements used by different regions, eventually leading to the establishment of the imperial system in the early 19th century. Over time, the fluid ounce has become a staple in both domestic and commercial settings, particularly in the UK and Commonwealth countries.
